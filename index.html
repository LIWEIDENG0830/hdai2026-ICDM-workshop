Overview

Human mobility data, together with broader human behavioral data generated from online platforms and human–AI interactions, provide rich signals for understanding human dynamics in both physical and digital environments. HDMA 2026 aims to provide a focused forum for discussing recent advances, open challenges, and practical applications at the intersection of human dynamics analytics, spatio-temporal intelligence, and agentic AI.

The workshop will be organized as a half-day event at ICDM 2026. It is intended to bring together researchers from data mining, machine learning, urban computing, and related communities to exchange ideas on modeling, reasoning, and decision-making over complex human-centered data.

Call for Papers

We invite original research contributions that advance the understanding, modeling, and utilization of human mobility data and broader human dynamics in the emerging landscape of large models and agentic AI.

Objectives and Goals

  • Bring together researchers and practitioners working on human mobility, broader human dynamics, and agentic AI.
  • Promote discussion on new methodologies, systems, and applications for analyzing complex human-centered data.
  • Highlight opportunities and open challenges in combining spatio-temporal data mining with large models and agentic reasoning.
  • Foster interdisciplinary exchange across data mining, machine learning, urban computing, and geospatial analytics.

Topics of Interest

  • Modeling and understanding of human mobility
  • Mobility forecasting and anomaly detection
  • Multi-agent simulation of mobility, interaction, diffusion, coordination, and population dynamics
  • Representation learning for mobility trajectories
  • Agentic reasoning, planning, and tool use over multimodal human-centered data
  • Large models for human mobility data understanding
  • Mobility foundation models and large mobility datasets
  • Agentic AI for mobility reasoning and decision making
  • Retrieval-augmented, memory-augmented, and long-context systems for behavior mining
  • Human-in-the-loop agents for decision support, recommendation, and intervention design
  • Applications in health, smart cities, transportation, workplaces, education, disaster response, and online platforms
